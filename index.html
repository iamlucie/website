Frequently Asked Questions

Designer DNA. I really enjoy working on strategic and intertwined problems, but I also think it would be a bad move to miss out on the latest technological developments — and those are typically best caught from a super-senior IC role, shaping what gets built at the very beginning and designing it with engineering. If my role would be mostly people management or technical backlog ownership, I'd probably still feel the calling… :)

I lean toward expert research and dev-like exploration with peer reviews over workshop-heavy ideation. I build consensus through early SME consultations and targeted alignment workshops — powerful reveal presentations only when necessary. I've run plenty of team workshops, but after five years remote I know there are people with regular practice, and I'm fine in the passenger seat on those.

Just that I don't like obvoious buzzword-dropping. I use AI in how I work. I learn towards self-hosted, sustainable, and privacy-respecting stack. I look for new and renewed interaction patterns related to it. And I enjoy watching some old good experience drift the current pace of work causes 🍿.

My recent and largest design topic in this area has been AI-automation platform explainability for people from understaffed sectors - skeptical, risk-averse, no centaurs or cyborgs, yet wanting to try this as a solution for being burried in communication and filling clunky forms.

I'm based in Dresden and not relocating. Remote works well as long as there's solid CET overlap or a strong async culture. Hybrid is fine if the commute makes sense — no problem to be in Berlin, Leipzig, or Prague once or twice a month. Full on-site only works here in Dresden. I work in English and German; for precision stuff I default to English, but most teams are fine with the mix.

For super-IC role, I'm looking for my next full-time or near full-time (80%+) opportunity, but open to contract or fractional if the fit is simply right. I'd rather talk about the role first — happy to align on comp once we both know what we're actually solving for.
